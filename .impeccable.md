## Design Context

### Users
Mariano — a backend programmer, casual/beginner investor. Single user, checks this dashboard occasionally (not continuously monitoring) to decide whether it's worth looking closer at a stock before buying. No live trading happens here; it's pure research support. He doesn't want to babysit spreadsheets or emails — this dashboard is the one place he looks.

### Brand Personality
Personal research journal, not a SaaS product. Three words: **considered, quiet, own-voice**. It should feel like a notebook Mariano built for himself — has personality and point of view, not a template anyone could have shipped. Dark mode, night-checking, low-key "trading desk at home" feeling rather than corporate fintech polish.

### Aesthetic Direction
Dark theme. Avoid generic SaaS dashboard tropes explicitly: no identical card grids, no rounded-icon-above-heading pattern, no glassmorphism, no gradient text/accents, no Bootstrap-y card shadows. Favor editorial/journal composition — asymmetric layout, clear typographic hierarchy, one or two well-chosen accent colors tinted rather than neon-on-dark. Data density should feel intentional: this is a short watchlist (~13 tickers), not a 200-row trading terminal, so give each ticker room to read as a small "entry" rather than cramming into a generic table.

### Design Principles
1. Feels handmade for one person, not templated for anyone — avoid anything that reads as "AI dashboard #4172."
2. Dark, tinted neutrals (no pure black); one deliberate accent hue used sparingly for signal, not decoration.
3. Typography carries hierarchy — distinctive display face for tickers/headers, refined body face for the "momento" narrative text.
4. Signals (MACD cross, RSI extremes, drawdown depth) should read at a glance without needing a legend, but never via decorative sparklines.
5. Empty/no-signal states should still feel informative ("nada que mirar hoy" reads differently than a blank table).
